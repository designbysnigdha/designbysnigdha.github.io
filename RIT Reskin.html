RIT UNIVERSITY NEWS RESKIN

There’s a secret ingredient to this growth and appeal: RIT's news website was infused with a bold branding. With the brand’s strong accessibility, its Oswald font, and blocked sections, this site captures the boldness of RIT.

Goals With This Project

The goal for the RIT University News website is to inform what is going on with RIT. It should increase awareness of RIT news and participation of RIT Events, increase the number of web page visitors, and increase duration of each visit.

I wanted my reskinning of the webpage to be visually engaging, captivating, and informative. The look should be refreshing, have strong visual hierarchy, support reading behavior (scanning, jumping, sustained reading) with proper legibility.

Content Hierarchy


Taking a clean navigation and the target audience into consideration, I developed this content hierarchy. I developed this to help organize the page in a way that makes it easy for readers to find the content they are looking for and understand the relationships between different pieces of the elements.

Iterations


  • I started this project by blocking out boxes to represent text and images. Using a grid system, I organized each element of the website down to the pixel. I started with a light blue theme with a full screen header, but quickly realized that I wanted to take it to another level in terms of mood and atmosphere. I stuck with a color pallette that matches closely to RIT'S orange branding, but chose a strong Slab Serif font. I sorted through many different news websites like The Times and Wired to understand what features were most user friendly. After perfecting my content hierarchy and positioning of elements, I started focusing on smaller nuances like adding the weather of the campus location and making small icons.
